Prepare Yourself For Market Changes

  • 19 July 2020
  • Benji Boom
  • 2 Comments

Due to recent troubles of coronavirus and stock market meltdown, U.S. investors have sold much of their portfolio to avoid further loss. Yet, the stock marketâ€™s 2009-2020 upswing, has produced capital gains for the investor, despite selling at a lower price.

Rather than pay a capital gains tax, why not reinvest the money into a Qualified Opportunity Fund? It offers a 5-10 year investment that can average 15% return per year. Plus, your money grows in U.S.-based real estate, backed by government incentives.

Once the coronavirus caseload hits a plateau, the stock market might see a major snap-back rally. This would mean stocks are overbought, and the market would go down again. Consider your short-term and long-term capital gains with this in mind. You can lessen equity exposure by rolling these gains into a Qualified Opportunity Fund within 180 days, which entitles you to defer taxes on those gains out to 2026.
